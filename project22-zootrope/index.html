Sound Design for a Digital Art Installation

«Art In Space» is part of the international digital art project, Project22, based in Dubai. Project22 aims to showcase the diversity of digital art and support artists by providing them with a platform for development and the exchange of ideas.

The «Art In Space» artwork is a modern interpretation of a zoetrope ‚Äì an antique device that creates the illusion of movement. The installation consists of 22 static images of horses that «come to life» when rotated, creating an animation effect. Conceptually, the work reflects the treatment of animals as objects, similar to beloved toys, which resonates with the theme of innovation and rethinking familiar things.

Implementation:

My task was to create a cohesive soundscape that not only accompanied the rotation of the zoetrope but also revealed its conceptual depth, enhancing the effect of the static images «coming to life» and immersing the viewer in the atmosphere of the installation. To achieve this, I used a comprehensive approach that included: Spatial audio techniques were employed to create the sensation that the sound is emanating from different points within the installation, enhancing the immersive effect. The music incorporates children’s musical instruments to emphasize its «toy-like» nature, and sounds of the mechanism’s rotation were created.
